Regular programme

Our regular programme includes world cinema, archive, experimental and much more…

African Odysseys

Inspirational films by and about the people of Africa, from archive classics to new cinema and docs. Programmed by David Somerset.

Download a pdf of the July and August African Odysseys screenings and events

  • African Odysseys Present: European Premiere: The Milkmaid + Q&A with producer Oluseun Sowemimo, hosted by Marcia Degia

    A striking, sensitive drama about two young Nigerian abductees who are caught in a life and death struggle for survival.

  • Black Lens Festival Opening Night: UK Premiere: How to Stop a Recurring Dream + Q&A with director Ed Morris and actor Ruby Barker

    A high-octane thriller in which a young woman kidnaps her younger sister and goes on the run.

  • Mandabi

    This landmark film from Ousmane Sembène takes a satirical swipe at poverty, bureaucracy and colonialism.

  • African Odysseys present: A Warm December + intro by actor Esther Anderson

    A rather unusual romantic comedy with serious themes, starring and directed by Sidney Poitier.

BFI Flare

LGBTQ+ films and events.

  • Happy Together

    Tony Leung and Leslie Cheung have smouldering chemistry as a couple falling out of love.

  • All About My Mother

    Pedro Almodóvar’s stylish and touching portrait of a woman’s attempts to come to terms with grief.

Experimenta

Artist film and alternative moving-image culture; works that break with convention. Programmed by William Fowler.

  • Born in Flames + discussion

    The revolution has come but old patriarchal prejudices remain – it’s time to take action.

  • Thundercrack! + intro by Jane Giles, author of ‘Scala Cinema 1978-1993’

    A delicious, high-camp celebration of sexuality, soundtracked by thunder.

Projecting the Archive

A rare chance to see rediscovered British features from the BFI National Archive. Programmed by Josephine Botting. In honour of Dr Clifford Shaw.

  • Trottie True (aka The Gay Lady) + intro by BFI Curator Josephine Botting

    A Gaiety girl turns duchess in this Technicolor homage to the music hall.

Seniors

Free and paid matinees, and talks for the over-60s. Programmed by David Somerset.

  • Seniors’ paid matinee: Nashville

    Altman’s magnum opus set in the country music capital is a witty and exhilarating portrait of modern America.

  • Seniors’ free archive matinee: Cabin in the Sky + intro by writer Marcus Powell

    A joyous gospel reworking of Faust and The Wizard of Oz with an all-star cast.

  • Seniors’ matinee: Mandabi + intro by film curator, programmer and BFI London Film Festival Programme Advisor, Keith Shiri

    This landmark film from Ousmane Sembène takes a satirical swipe at poverty, bureaucracy and colonialism.

  • Seniors’ free matinee: Personal Services + intro and Q&A with author, Graham Rinaldi

    A drama about the life and times of a 1980s society madam.

  • Seniors’ matinee: Pool of London + intro

    Two sailors go on shore leave and pursue romance in this London-set drama.

Silent Cinema

Enjoy the best international restorations, often with a live musical accompaniment. Programmed by Bryony Dixon.

  • Nell Gwyn + intro by BFI Curator Bryony Dixon

    A delicious regal romp starring the other Gish sister, Dorothy.

  • The Wit and Wisdom of A.A. Milne + intro by Bryony Dixon, BFI Curator

    A triple bill of rare A. A. Milne films from the creator of Winnie-the-Pooh.

Sonic Cinema

Music-inspired films and events. Programmed by Tim Stevens.

  • Sonic Cinema: World Premiere: What The Hell Is It This Time? Sparks Live in London + intro by Edgar Wright

    Edgar Wright directs this full-length live Sparks concert, featuring 22 tracks from 5 decades.

Terror Vision

The darker side of cinema; films that will horrify and astound. Programmed by Michael Blyth.

  • Children Shouldn’t Play With Dead Things

    A pseudo-satanic ritual on an abandoned island starts out as fun and games but…

  • Dr Terror’s House of Horrors

    Christopher Lee and Peter Cushing star in this iconic anthology horror from Amicus Productions.

Woman with a Movie Camera

We celebrate women’s contribution to cinema and spotlight female stories.

  • Woman with a Movie Camera Summit powered by Jaguar

    Reframing the conversation around women and film.

  • Censor + Q&A with director Prano Bailey-Bond

    A frightening but fun homage to ‘video nasties’ and classic horror.

  • World Restoration Premiere of Riddance + pre-recorded extended intro by Márta Mészáros

    A thoughtful, observational drama about a young woman’s fight to retain her integrity despite falling in love.

  • Woman With a Movie Camera Preview: Girlfriends

    A struggling photographer feels cast adrift after her roommate moves out to get married.

  • Preview: Souad + pre-recorded Q&A with director Ayten Amin

    An engrossing portrait of Middle Eastern Generation Z.
